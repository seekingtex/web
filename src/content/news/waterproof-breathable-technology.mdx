Waterproof breathable fabrics are engineered materials that prevent water penetration while allowing moisture vapor to escape. This seemingly contradictory capability is achieved through sophisticated material science.

## Key Technologies

### 1. Coated Fabrics

A waterproof coating (typically PU) is applied to the fabric surface. Water column ratings of 3000-5000mm are standard. Best for entry to mid-level outerwear.

### 2. Membrane Laminates

A thin waterproof breathable membrane (PU or ePTFE) is laminated to the fabric. Water column ratings of 8000-20000mm. Used in premium outerwear and professional gear.

### 3. 2-Layer vs 3-Layer Construction

- **2-Layer**: Outer fabric + membrane, with a separate lining
- **3-Layer**: Outer fabric + membrane + inner lining bonded together. More durable, lighter, but more expensive.

## Choosing the Right Fabric

Consider these factors when selecting waterproof fabric:

- **Intended use**: Casual rainwear vs alpine climbing
- **Breathability needs**: Higher activity = higher breathability requirement
- **Durability requirements**: Abrasion resistance for tactical vs lightweight for running
- **Environmental standards**: PFAS-free DWR options available
