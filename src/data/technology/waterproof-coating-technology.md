Seekingtex waterproof coating technology applies polymer coatings to fabric substrates to create impermeable barriers against water penetration. Three primary methods are used depending on performance requirements and end-use application.

PU (polyurethane) coatings are applied via direct coating or transfer coating processes. A thin film of PU solution is spread onto the fabric surface and cured. PU-coated fabrics offer good waterproofness (5000-15000mm hydrostatic head) with moderate breathability.

TPU lamination uses heat and pressure to bond a pre-formed TPU film to the fabric. This method provides superior adhesion strength and durability compared to wet coating. TPU-laminated fabrics achieve 10000-20000mm hydrostatic head ratings.

All Seekingtex waterproof coatings are tested for hydrostatic head per AATCC 127 or ISO 811. Applications include rainwear, ski wear, outdoor shells, workwear, and protective clothing.
